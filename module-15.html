15-minute session / Retrieval practice

Retrieve, fix and plan.

You bring the content. You write down what you know, hide it, pull it back from memory, repair what was missing, and schedule when to come back. Any subject. Any topic.

01 — Setup

What are you revising?

Write 3–5 key ideas from your notes in your own words — not copied directly. Short phrases are fine. These become your study material for this session.

Aim for 3–5 ideas. Start with the ones you find hardest to remember.

Write at least two ideas before you start.

02 — Study

Read for meaning, not just the words.

Look for how the ideas connect. You can move on when the structure makes sense — not just when the words look familiar.

      07 — Plan

      Space out your return.

      Retrieving this again after a gap builds stronger memory than re-reading it now. The wait is what makes the learning stick.

      Why spacing works: when you retrieve something after a short delay, partial forgetting means you have to work harder to recall it. That extra effort strengthens the memory trace more than studying when everything feels fresh.

      Choose a realistic time and note it in your revision planner so you don't forget.

      When will you try this again?

      Choose a realistic return time before you finish.
